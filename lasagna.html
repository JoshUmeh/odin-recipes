Description

This classic lasagna builds deep, savory flavor by starting with a robust meat sauce. Sauté finely chopped onion and garlic until fragrant, then brown a mixture of ground beef and Italian sausage. Stir in crushed San Marzano tomatoes, tomato paste, and a generous pinch of dried oregano and basil, allowing the sauce to simmer for at least thirty minutes until thickened. This slow cooking melds the ingredients and creates a rich, hearty foundation that is essential to the dish.

The assembly is key to achieving the perfect balance of textures. Combine ricotta cheese with an egg, Parmesan, and parsley to create a stable, creamy layer. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce, then top with overlapping no-boil lasagna noodles. Spread a portion of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by a layer of shredded low-moisture mozzarella, and then more meat sauce. Repeat these layers, finishing with a final blanket of mozzarella and Parmesan that bakes into a golden, bubbly crust.

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce:

  • tablespoon olive oil
  • large yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio is ideal)
  • ½ pound mild or sweet Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For the Cheese Filling:

  • 16 ounces (about 2 cups) whole-milk ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped

For Assembly:

  • 12 no-boil (oven-ready) lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces (about 4 cups) low-moisture, whole-milk mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Additional ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese for the top

Steps

For Sauce:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, sauté 1 finely chopped onion and 2-3 minced garlic cloves until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add 1 lb ground beef and ½ lb Italian sausage (casings removed) to the pot. Cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until fully browned. Drain excess fat.
  3. Stir in 1 (28 oz) can of crushed San Marzano tomatoes, 2 tablespoons of tomato paste, 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, and 1 teaspoon of dried basil.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let the sauce cook for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

For the Cheese Filling:

  1. While the sauce simmers, in a separate bowl, mix 16 oz of ricotta cheese, 1 large egg, ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley until well combined.

To Assemble:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  3. Place a layer of no-boil lasagna noodles over the sauce, slightly overlapping them.
  4. Spread one-third of the ricotta cheese mixture evenly over the noodles.
  5. Sprinkle a generous handful (about 1 cup) of shredded low-moisture mozzarella cheese over the ricotta.
  6. Spoon another layer of meat sauce over the mozzarella.
  7. Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, mozzarella, sauce) two more times.
  8. For the final layer, top with the remaining sauce and a final, generous sprinkling of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.

To Bake:

  1. Cover the dish tightly with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  2. Remove the foil and continue baking for another 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and the sauce is bubbling around the edges.
  3. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving. This allows the layers to set for cleaner portions.
