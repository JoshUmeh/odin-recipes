Afang Soup

Home

Description

Afang Soup is a traditional Nigerian soup from the Efik and Ibibio people of Southern Nigeria. It's made primarily with afang leaves (a wild vine leaf with a slightly bitter taste) and waterleaf, cooked with various proteins in a flavorful palm oil-based broth.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 4 cups sliced afang leaves (fresh or dried)
  • 6 cups waterleaf, chopped
  • 500g assorted meat (beef, goat meat, or cow foot)
  • 200g dried fish (stockfish or dried catfish)
  • 200g fresh fish (tilapia or mackerel)
  • 1 cup periwinkles or snails (optional)
  • 2 cups stock (from cooked meat)

Seasonings & Thickeners:

  • 3 tablespoons palm oil
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, blended
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 stock cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ogiri (traditional seasoning, optional)

Steps

  1. Wash and season meat with onion, stock cube, and salt
  2. Cook meat until tender (about 30-40 minutes), reserve stock
  3. Soak dried fish in hot water to soften, remove bones
  4. Clean fresh fish, season lightly, and set aside
  5. Clean snails/periwinkles if using

Prepare the Leaves:

  1. Wash afang leaves thoroughly (if fresh, slice thinly)
  2. If using dried afang leaves, soak in warm water for 10 minutes
  3. Wash waterleaf and chop coarsely
  4. Pound or blend afang leaves until finely shredded (traditional method uses mortar and pestle)

Start Cooking the Soup:

  1. In a large pot, heat palm oil for 1 minute on medium heat
  2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent
  3. Add blended pepper and cook for 3-4 minutes

Combine Ingredients:

  1. Add cooked meat and its stock to the pot
  2. Add dried fish, crayfish, remaining stock cube, and ogiri (if using)
  3. Add waterleaf and stir well
  4. Cook for 5-7 minutes until waterleaf wilts

Add Afang Leaves:

  1. Add the pounded afang leaves to the pot
  2. Stir thoroughly to combine
  3. Add fresh fish and snails/periwinkles if using
  4. Add water if needed to achieve desired consistency

Final Simmering:

  1. Cover and simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes
  2. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking
  3. Adjust seasoning with salt if needed
  4. The soup should thicken slightly from the leaves
