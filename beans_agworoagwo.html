Beans Agworoagwo

Home

Description

Beans Agworoagwo is a hearty Nigerian beans porridge that originates from the Igbo ethnic group. It's a comfort food staple made by cooking beans until they become soft and creamy, then simmering them in a flavorful blend of palm oil, peppers, onions, and traditional seasonings. The dish gets its distinctive name from the cooking process where the beans are stirred and mashed to create a thick, porridge-like consistency.

What sets Agworoagwo apart is its rich, earthy flavor profile from the palm oil and the gentle heat from scotch bonnet peppers. Often enjoyed as a complete meal, it's typically served with garri (cassava flakes), bread, or fried plantains. This nutritious dish is not only filling but also represents the cultural heritage of Nigerian home cooking, where simple ingredients transform into deeply satisfying meals.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 2 cups honey beans or black-eyed peas
  • 1 medium red onion, chopped
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo)
  • 3 tablespoons palm oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish
  • 1 stock cube (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for cooking

Optional Additions

  • 2 cups chopped spinach or bitter leaf (scent leaf)
  • 1 tablespoon dried fish (flaked)
  • 1 smoked fish (deboned and flaked)
  • 1 tablespoon ground dried shrimp

Steps

Preparation:

  1. Pick through the beans to remove any stones or debris
  2. Rinse the beans thoroughly in cold water
  3. Soak the beans in water for 2-3 hours (optional, reduces cooking time)

Cooking the Beans:

  1. Drain the soaked beans and transfer to a large pot
  2. Add enough water to cover the beans by about 2 inches
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium
  4. Cook for 45-60 minutes until beans are very soft (add more water if needed)

Prepare Pepper Mix:

  1. While beans are cooking, wash and blend the red bell peppers and scotch bonnet peppers into a smooth paste
  2. Chop the onion finely

Combine Ingredients:

  1. When beans are soft, add the blended pepper mix to the pot
  2. Add chopped onions and palm oil
  3. Stir well to combine

Seasoning:

  1. Add ground crayfish, crumbled stock cube, and salt to taste
  2. If using dried fish or smoked fish, add it now
  3. Stir thoroughly

Final Cooking:

  1. Continue cooking for another 15-20 minutes on medium heat
  2. Stir occasionally to prevent burning
  3. The porridge should thicken as it cooks

Add Greens (Optional):

  1. If using spinach or bitter leaf, add it in the last 5 minutes of cooking
  2. Stir gently to incorporate

Finish:

  1. Cook until oil floats to the top (sign it's ready)
  2. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed
  3. The consistency should be thick but not dry
